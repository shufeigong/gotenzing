POSITIONING

Years ago, two smart guys (Al Ries & Jack Trout) wrote a book about positioning. In a world now filled to the ozone with undifferentiated, competing products and services, and endless, fragmented media tactics, their advice is now biblical. We suggest you either practice the art of positioning for preference through differentiation or prepare to drop your margins.

Tenzing has created unique positioning strategies in financial services, business and professional services, technology, healthcare, economic development, consumer products and retail. Oddly enough, though, most of our relationships don't start there. A prospective client typically starts by asking us to make an ad or shoot a film or produce a web site. That reflects the current state of our industry - everyone wants the tactical stuff. We respond with a few questions; like what will make the ad, the film or the web site unique? What's your value proposition? Do your staff know your value proposition? The light comes on, we step back together, we find the position that results in preference - and then we produce the ads, the films and the web sites. Funny how that works (out so well).
